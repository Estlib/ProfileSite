= Universal Recipes

This page contains a list of universal recipes used in many of the recipes in the recipe book



01 - Salty cookie base dough.

Ingredients:

  • all purpose flour: 200g
  • butter: 150g
  • salt: to taste, but at least half a teaspoon
  • ice cold water: 30g
  • Addins as per specified by the recipe that refers here for the base dough

For lactose intolerant people: you can replace butter 1:1 with any sort of solid fat except coconut fat.

add all the ingredients except for the addins to the bowl, cut up the butter beforehand
and work it into a doughball. the ball of dough should be relatively soft and pliable,
but hold its shape and not smear. add the addins and work it into a uniform dough ball.
refrigerate for one hour. make into cookies and bake for 7 minutes at 200c.

baking time is not absolute, it may take faster or slower for you,
but the cookies should be very slightly darker at the edges to know
when they are done visually.

the dough makes for airy cookies, some may form bubbles in it.
the cookies do not spread while baking, they will shrink slightly when cooling.




02 - Sweet cookie dough

this dough has multiple uses:

  • Hard Tart base
  • Sweet pie crust
  • Cookies
  • Gingerbreads

Ingredients:

  • all purpose flour: 400g
  • butter: 200g
  • sugar: 200g
  • egg: one
  • Addins as per specified by the recipe that refers here for the base dough

For lactose intolerant people: you can replace butter 1:1 with any sort of solid fat except coconut fat.
For diverging dough usage, the sugar can be replaced 1:1 with any sort of other sugar product or natura sugar substitute.
These being: sugar, molasses, brown sugar, caster sugar, honey, fructose.
The sugars can also be mixed and matched, in both: type and state. you can mix solid sugars with liquid ones.

Into a large bowl, add the flour and sugar. make a well in the center for the egg and
cut up the butter, after which you put butter pieces around the egg. Mix and form the dough
into a ball. the dough should be stiff and not too pliable. the bowl should also come clean
after it has fully formed. After that, refrigerate the dough for atleast half an hour.
There is no maximum refrigeration time, as this dough can be frozen in the ice box for
later usage. I dont recommend to freeze it for over a week. for immediate use, do not
refrigerate in the ice box, just normal fridge area. After refrigeration, please refer
to the forming instructions in the recipe which refers to this recipe.


baking time is not absolute, it may take faster or slower for you,
but the cookies should be very slightly darker at the edges to know
when they are done visually.

the dough makes for medium-dense cookies, bubbles dont normally appear with proper dough
reforming. the cookies do not spread while baking, they dont shrink when cooling.
