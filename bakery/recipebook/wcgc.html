Woven Chocolate Glaze Cookies



Dough: 02 Click here the base dough recipe.

Ingredients:

  • all purpose flour: 400g
  • butter: 200g
  • sugar: 200g
  • egg: one
  • Vanilla: to taste, can be either sugar form or extract. any works
  • Brown Glaze from xocofine. More on this below.

For lactose intolerant people: you can replace butter 1:1 with any sort of solid fat except coconut fat.
For diverging dough usage, the sugar can be replaced 1:1 with any sort of other sugar product or natura sugar substitute.
These being: sugar, molasses, brown sugar, caster sugar, honey, fructose.
The sugars can also be mixed and matched, in both: type and state. you can mix solid sugars with liquid ones.
For the glaze, while the recipe specifies a specific product, if you are proficient in chocolate or couverture tempering
i recommend you go for that instead. I am using this because its foolproof. no tempering needed and it hardens pretty fast.

Into a large bowl, add the flour and sugar. make a well in the center for the egg and
cut up the butter, after which you put butter pieces around the egg. Mix and form the dough
into a ball. the dough should be stiff and not too pliable. the bowl should also come clean
after it has fully formed. After that, refrigerate the dough for atleast half an hour.
There is no maximum refrigeration time, as this dough can be frozen in the ice box for
later usage. I dont recommend to freeze it for over a week. for immediate use, do not
refrigerate in the ice box, just normal fridge area. After refrigeration, roll out the dough
on a table, the thickness of the dough is up to you, based on what you can handle. You can
go thinner for better veawing control or thicker for a thicker cookie. I recommend going
thicker, as thin ones burn really easy. After rolling out the dough, make the strips roughly
5mm wide, and between 8-12 cm in length, prepare 2 small parchment pieces, on one of them,
put some flour on it and weave 5 strips horisontally and 5 vertically, this size should be
big enough for a large circle cutter. dont forget to press lightly on the "joints" for
a proper adherance, it is important when transferring the circle onto the baking tray.
after your weaving is complete, cut the circle out, but dont remove the cutter after pressing
the cookie out, before you lift it away, remove the residual dough around it. Then lift the
cookie cutter. This ensures that the shape is preserved before lifting, as removing the
excess afterwards can warp and distort the brittle shape. this is a finicky cookie to make.
Transferring happens with the help of the other small parchment piece. make sure that the
other parchment piece is floured too, place the other piece ontop of the one your weaving is on
hold it in your hand and flip it over. The cookie should flip onto the other piece relatively easy
if it doesnt, some peeling may be required. If it does happen that you need to peel it, peel it
gently with the edge of a knife, and flour the parchment paper well afterwards. once the cookie
is on the other parchment, move it over the baking tray, place it onto the tray and either:
slide it off gently with minimal finger involvement or yank the parchment piece out from it
in one fast motion. see which one works better for you, but in either case, if you need to
reposition the cookie on the tray a bit, then you need to move it with two fingers, taking
hold of the cookie from the centermost "joint" and moving it gently that way.
Congratulations - your cookie is now on the tray. Bake it for 5-7 minutes at 200c
after you have all of your cookies baked, it is time to partially enrobe them in
glaze. if you have your own method for this, feel free to diverge from these instructions
but for the rest of you, melt the xocofine glaze on a waterbath type of setup. im just using
my kettle and a bowl directly on the stove to do this, so you dont need special equiptment
either. not an electric kettle mind you, the old fashioned type of water kettle.
the glaze will not run on its own, you need to spoon it on if you have a shallow setup
and you also might need something to empty the holes manually, as sometimes the glaze
is thick enough to close those too. place the freshly glazed cookie on some parchment paper
or other nonstick surface to cool and harden. once the hardening is complete, you have your
glorious woven cookies. one dough took me an entire day to do. its the weaving that takes
most of the time to do


baking time is not absolute, it may take faster or slower for you, but for this recipe,
i reccommend you premake all the trayfuls because this cookie bakes FAST. 5 minutes is on
the high side once your oven gets going at 200c. but as per usual, they are done when the cookies
are very slightly darker at the edges. they also need to cool on the tray, as these WILL
crumble if moved when warm and not set. The reason they bake fast is that due to the holes
in the weaving, it has more surface area enabling a fast baking time.


the dough makes for crunchy cookies, bubbles dont normally appear with proper dough
reforming. the cookies do not spread while baking, they dont shrink when cooling.
